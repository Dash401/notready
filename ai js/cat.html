Cats

Lymphoma


Lymphoma is a cancer of the lymphocytes. Lymphocytes are cells that are involved in the immune system.

Fibrosarcoma


Fibrosarcoma is a locally aggressive cancer of the soft tissues (typically skin) that is relatively common in cats.

Squamous Cell Carcinoma


A squamous cell carcinoma is a type of cancer that originates in the squamous epithelium.

High Rise Syndrome


High-rise syndrome is the phenomenon of cats falling from higher than two stories (7–9 m (23–30 ft)).

Ringworm


Ringworm lesions create hairless, scaly, red rings on the skin. They're also often quite itchy.

Feline Immunodeficiency Virus


Feline immunodeficiency virus is a lentivirus that affects cats worldwide, with 2.5% to 4.4% of felines being infected.

Leptospirosis


Leptospirosis is an infectious disease that causes serious illness in dogs, other animals.

Upper Respiratory Infections


A sneezing cat or a coughing cat can signal an upper respiratory infection.
